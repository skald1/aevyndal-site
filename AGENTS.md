# Aevyndal Project Directive

Technical decisions must follow `ARCHITECTURE.md`, component decisions must follow `COMPONENT_LIBRARY.md`, card decisions must follow `CARD_SYSTEM.md`, section composition must follow `SECTION_SYSTEM.md`, interactions must follow `INTERACTION_SYSTEM.md`, search must follow `SEARCH_SYSTEM.md`, motion decisions must follow `MOTION_SYSTEM.md`, transitions must follow `TRANSITION_SYSTEM.md`, media decisions must follow `MEDIA_SYSTEM.md`, age atmosphere decisions must follow `AGE_VISUAL_SYSTEM.md`, visual constants must follow `DESIGN_TOKENS.md`, implementation sequencing must follow `HOMEPAGE_BUILD_ORDER.md`, content relationships must follow `CONTENT_MODEL.md`, writing structures must follow `ARTICLE_TEMPLATES.md`, and discovery decisions must follow `SEO_STRATEGY.md`. When an implementation choice is uncertain, use the simpler static solution that best supports the experience described here.

The site's primary mission is to help people imagine who they could be in Aevyndal.

When priorities conflict, use this order: identity, belonging, relationships, character, story, setting, lore, mechanics.

Before publishing content, ask: "How does this help someone imagine who they could be in Aevyndal?" If the answer is unclear, revise.

## Content

- Begin with what it is like to be one of these people, then explain what the subject is.
- Favor daily life, beliefs, relationships, duties, opportunities, tensions, and present consequences.
- Use history to explain the present. Avoid encyclopedia structure unless it is explicitly requested.
- Articles should move through: what it is, what it is like, why it matters, who lives here, what threatens it, what stories happen here, then history.
- Preserve existing work and redirect gradually. Do not invalidate established decisions.

## Experience

- The website is a living archive, not an encyclopedia.
- Design around visitor questions: what is this, what stories exist here, put me somewhere, who could I be, tell me more, can I use this, can I belong, can I contribute.
- People begin with places. Identity comes before lore; belonging before information. Provide doors before walls of information.
- Images should show belonging, community, ritual, work, travel, discovery, celebration, and mourning before combat or spectacle.

## Design Check

Before finalizing, be able to answer: Who does this create? How do they live? What do they value? What pressures shape them? What relationships define them? What challenges them? Why would someone want to portray, follow, or become them?

Mechanics support identity. They must not become the reason an identity exists.
