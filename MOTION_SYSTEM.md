# ÆVYNDAL MOTION SYSTEM

## Purpose

Motion exists to make the world feel alive.

Motion does not exist to impress.

Motion should be felt before it is noticed.

Everything should drift.

Nothing should demand attention.

---

# Primary Principle

Atmosphere transitions continuously.

Information transitions discretely.

The user should never feel they have left the world.

They should feel the world changed around them.

---

# Motion Philosophy

Avoid:

* spectacle
* bounce
* overshoot
* spring physics
* flashy effects
* loading screens

Prefer:

* fading
* drifting
* dissolving
* breathing
* crossfades
* gradual reveals

---

# Motion Hierarchy

World

↓

Atmosphere

↓

Transition Layer

↓

Glass

↓

Information

---

# Timing

## Information

250–500ms

Examples:

* labels
* titles
* descriptions
* metadata

Should enter and leave gracefully.

---

## Cards

400–700ms

Examples:

* card appearance
* hover states
* image transitions

Cards should feel weightless.

---

## Atmosphere

1000–1500ms

Examples:

* age transitions
* background changes

The world changes slowly.

---

## Ambient Motion

Continuous

Examples:

* fog
* stars
* rain
* pollen
* snow

Almost imperceptible.

---

# Age Transition Principle

Changing ages should not feel like:

Theme switching.

Changing pages.

Loading.

Instead:

It should feel like travel.

---

# Continuous Transition

Old atmosphere remains.

New atmosphere gradually emerges.

Elements leave individually.

Elements arrive individually.

Nothing vanishes simultaneously.

---

# Text Motion

Text transitions independently.

Old text:

* fades
* lowers slightly
* loses opacity

New text:

* enters later
* rises slightly
* gains opacity

Text should never pop.

---

# Image Motion

Images crossfade.

Cards inherit new imagery.

No hard cuts.

No slide carousels.

Images are memories.

Not screens.

---

# Hover Motion

Very subtle.

Scale:

1.01–1.03

Never larger.

Avoid exaggerated zoom.

Cards should remain calm.

---

# Navigation Motion

Floating navigation:

Always present.

Ghost header:

Appears gradually.

Never snaps.

Should feel inevitable.

---

# Search Motion

Search opens softly.

Background dims.

No full-screen takeovers.

Search should feel inviting.

Not disruptive.

---

# Page Transitions

Prefer View Transitions API.

Maintain:

* cards
* navigation
* shell

Atmosphere changes.

Information changes.

The world remains.

---

# Particles

Use sparingly.

Examples:

Fourth Age:

* fog
* snow
* drifting leaves

Seventh Age:

* stars
* rain
* lens artifacts

Eighth Age:

* pollen
* dust
* light bloom

Motion should remain subtle.

---

# Video Motion

Ambient video only.

Rules:

No cuts.

No action.

No dialogue.

No camera shake.

Slow loops.

5–12 seconds.

Darkened.

Desaturated.

Poster fallback required.

---

# Reduced Motion

Honor user preferences.

Disable:

* particles
* transitions
* ambient video

Fallback:

Static poster images.

The experience must remain beautiful.

Accessibility is mandatory.

---

# Avoid

No parallax abuse.

No carousels.

No animated counters.

No excessive blur.

No spinning objects.

No bounce effects.

No scroll-jacking.

No page loaders.

No fake physics.

---

# Desired Feeling

The world is alive.

The archive floats inside it.

People should not notice the motion.

They should simply feel comfortable there.

---

# Final Rule

Motion should create atmosphere.

Never distraction.

If an effect draws attention to itself, reduce it.
