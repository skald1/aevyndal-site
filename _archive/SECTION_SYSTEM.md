## Purpose

This document defines how sections behave throughout the site.

Pages should feel cohesive.

People should unconsciously understand where they are.

Every section should answer a question.

Never create sections merely to display information.

---

# Philosophy

Pages are conversations.

Sections answer questions.

The reader moves downward because curiosity pulls them.

Not because information is being dumped.

---

# Labels

Small.

Subtle.

Uppercase.

Examples:

LOCATION

CULTURE

CREATURE

THREATS

HISTORY

Never larger than the title.

Labels orient.

They do not dominate.

---

# Titles

Large.

Simple.

Human.

Avoid academic wording.

Good:

Daily Life

What People Fear

Beliefs

Stories

Bad:

Sociological Implications

Ecological Distribution

---

# Descriptions

One or two sentences.

Invite.

Do not summarize everything.

Leave curiosity alive.

---

# Content

Readable.

Short paragraphs.

Generous whitespace.

Never walls of text.

---

# Relationship Cards

Always end sections with:

"What else belongs here?"

Examples:

Nearby Places

Related Creatures

Stories

Campaigns

People

Faiths

The world should feel interconnected.

# Community Sections

---

# Visual Rhythm

Large section spacing.

Breathing room.

Alternating media emphasis.

Avoid identical patterns repeated endlessly.

---

# Section Backgrounds

Sections should not feel boxed.

Use:

Light gradients

Atmosphere

Images

Noise

Subtle shifts

Avoid heavy separators.

The page should feel continuous.

---

# Scrolling Philosophy

Scrolling should feel like walking.

Not paging.

Not consuming.

The reader descends into the world.

---

# Final Rule

Every section should answer:

"What question is the visitor asking right now?"

before asking:

"What information do we have to display?"
