# ÆVYNDAL CONTENT MODEL

## Purpose

This document defines how content is structured and related.

Ævyndal is not a wiki.

It is a graph.

Relationships matter more than categories.

The goal is discovery, not filing.

---

# Philosophy

People do not think in folders.

They think in connections.

A creature belongs to a place.

A place belongs to a people.

A people belongs to an age.

A story connects them.

Content should reflect this.

---

# Collections

## Ages

Represents major eras.

Examples:

* First Age
* Fourth Age
* Seventh Age

An age contains:

* locations
* peoples
* creatures
* articles
* campaigns

---

## Locations

Represents places.

Examples:

* Hallowmere
* Cultrek
* Kill City
* Greenmarch

A location connects to:

* age
* creatures
* peoples
* articles
* nearby locations
* campaigns

---

## Lineages

Represents ancestries and peoples.

Examples:

* th'il
* korvad'an
* t'nao

Connects to:

* locations
* articles
* cultures
* faiths
* characters

Mechanics are secondary.

Identity is primary.

---

## Creatures

Represents monsters and beings.

Examples:

* Rememberers
* Scarmakers

Connects to:

* locations
* stories
* articles
* cultures
* superstitions

Creatures reveal people.

Not merely ecosystems.

---

## Deities

Represents faith.

Connects to:

* peoples
* cultures
* locations
* rituals
* conflicts

Focus on worshippers.

Not divine statistics.

---

## Articles

General knowledge.

Flexible.

Connects to everything.

Articles are bridges.

Not destinations.

---

## Campaigns

Actual stories.

Examples:

* Seekers of the Fall
* Fragments
* The Returned

Connects to:

* people
* places
* creatures
* ages

Campaigns prove the world lives.

---

## People

Important individuals.

Connects to:

* places
* cultures
* faiths
* campaigns
* stories

Characters embody the world.

---

# Relationship Model

Age

↓

Location

↓

Culture

↓

People

↓

Stories

↓

Creatures

↓

Articles

All relationships should remain bidirectional.

Nothing exists in isolation.

---

# Article Structure

Every article answers:

1. What is it?
2. What is it like?
3. Why should I care?
4. Who lives here?
5. What threatens it?
6. What stories happen here?
7. History

History always comes last.

# Relationship Discovery

Related content should answer:

"What else belongs here?"

Never:

"More articles."

Connections should feel natural.

---

# Collections Philosophy

Content should support discovery.

Discovery should support identity.

Identity should support belonging.

Belonging is the purpose.

---

# Final Rule

Every piece of content should answer:

"Who could I be here?"

before:

"What information do I contain?"
